9 tabs is too many for a bottom bar (standard max is 5) — you'd need to group them. Here's how I'd approach it:

Proposed 5 primary tabs:

Tab	Icon	Contains
Home	home	Current home screen
Explore	compass	Discover + Activities + Places Near Me (sub-tabs at top)
Plan	calendar	Idea Generation + Date Calendar + Saved Ideas (sub-tabs at top)
Recipes	restaurant	Date Recipes
History	time	Date History
The two tabs with sub-content ("Explore" and "Plan") would use a top segmented control or chip row to switch between their inner screens — the same pattern Instagram uses for Reels/Photos or Spotify uses for Albums/Tracks.

Why this grouping:

Explore = things you browse to find something new
Plan = things tied to organizing and remembering dates
Main tradeoff: Activities, Places, Saved Ideas, Calendar, and Idea Generation each gain one extra tap to reach. That's the unavoidable cost of going from 9 to 5. The gain is a usable bottom bar that actually fits on screen without tiny icons and clipped labels.

Alternative if you want to avoid the sub-tab complexity: Drop "Places Near Me" (fold it into Activities or Discover as a filter), and drop "Date Activities" as a tab (fold it into Discover). That gets you to 6-7 naturally, then maybe merge History + Saved into one "My Dates" tab to land at 5.
